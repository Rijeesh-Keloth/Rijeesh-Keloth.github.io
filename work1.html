Dr. Rijeesh Keloth

Research Scientist (Virginia Tech). Experimental neutrino physics and dark-matter searches. I design and integrate detector systems and build data-driven analyses for neutrino oscillations, sterile-neutrino searches, and liquid-argon TPC experiments (DarkSIDE-20k).

Publications Talks CV (PDF) Google Scholar

Research Focus

Building precise experiments and robust analyses to probe physics beyond the Standard Model.

Current

  • DarkSIDE-20k (Virginia Tech): Hardware design, calibration, and integration for a liquid-argon dark-matter detector; developing QA workflows and precision components for stable long-term operation.
  • DUNE (Fermilab): A collaborator with a focus on proto-DUNE experiment located at the CERN.

Selected Prior Contributions

  • SoLid (BR2 Reactor): Led background studies using full reactor-off data for simulation tuning; oscillation fit & sensitivity; introduced first ML-based HNL signal discriminant; Data Manager; led Data Quality & Operations for 3+ years.
  • NOvA (Fermilab): First τ-appearance search for sterile oscillations in the Near Detector; built TMVA-based hadronic-τ IDs; produced datasets for 8×1020 POT analyses; APD QA; on-detector electronics replacement; control-room operations.

Background (short)

My work spans detector hardware & QA, Monte Carlo production/analysis, and experiment operations. I focus on analysis architectures that remain robust under detector/systematic effects and on hardware procedures that keep large detectors stable over long runs.

Future Interests

Precision instrumentation and ML-assisted analysis pipelines for next-gen neutrino and dark-matter experiments; scalable QA systems for LAr-TPCs; cross-experiment tooling that improves sensitivity and uptime.
